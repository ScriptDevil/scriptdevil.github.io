ยง Are we SPA yet?

[posted on 18 July 2020] (tag-satire, tag-blogging)

I did a tiny experiment a few days back. I checked how much I had accumulated in terms of notes over the past year and a bit. Turns out I had written less than 8MB of text - and this includes a lot of content that I had copy-pasted in.

Now, I thought of time I had wasted beautifying my previous blog. The amount of time I wasted optimizing how the output of my flavor-of-the-month static site generator/CSS framework looked. And for what? I don't know a single person other than me who read the blog.

That got me thinking. Why did I use a static site generator in the first place (other than the convenience of using markdown?). Turns out I had to find some way of templating the stuff around the actual articles I wrote. The bloat was a side-effect of me not having to focus on everything that was being served but only the content I wrote. The static assets on my previous attempt at a blog took over 4MB. I had only written 300kB of actual content in that site.

So the lightbulb went off, what if I didn't need to copy the template over and over into each post I wrote? What if ... there was only one page? Ooh! Aren't SPAs all the rage?

So there goes! Welcome to this SPA. Every post lives here. I have done by best not to cripple the site on purpose. I use sans-serif font because it is better on the eyes, but I don't force you to use a particular font. I have a maintain a list of posts at the top that you can use to navigate or jump to posts of your choice. I still have tags. Simply search for tag-NameOfTheTag. Each post heading will still be a link so that you can quickly copy a reference to the link if you want to.

Yes, it is an ugly beast. But it is my ugly beast! If you use a text-mode browser like w3m or links, you shouldn't notice a difference. If you really want to switch over fonts, background colors, width, or even voice narration(!), I would strongly urge you to use Mozilla Firefox and its excellent Reader View (which also works on your phone!)

Aggregators like Reddit, HN or Lobste.rs might have a hard time since the only part of the URL that changes between posts is the anchor (https://scriptdevil.in/#post-name). But then again, it isn't like I am making frontpage everyday. So who cares? There is also the problem of being indexed properly by search pages. Meh!

Once you load this page. You get to read everything I have written here offline! So, my genius has inadvertently added in another feature - we are not just an SPA, but also a PWA! Beat that.
